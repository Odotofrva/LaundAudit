3-Phase Execution

The first 90 days are about learning the store, setting the rhythm, and proving results.

This plan moves in three phases: first understand the location from the customer and team perspective, then build repeatable systems, then show measurable improvement through weekly reporting and action.

Days 1–30

Learn + Stabilize

Get close to the daily operation, identify what needs attention, and create a clean baseline.

  • Walk the store daily and observe customer flow, machine usage, staff routines, and peak periods.
  • Listen to staff and customers to understand repeat complaints, service gaps, and quick wins.
  • Start logs for machine downtime, complaints, refunds, supplies, cleaning checks, and WDF flow.
  • Build a baseline around sales, wash-dry-fold volume, labor fit, reviews, and cleanliness.
30-Day Output: Store baseline + top priorities + quick wins completed.
Days 31–60

Systemize + Coach

Turn expectations into clear shift standards and coach the team around measurable habits.

  • Create opening, mid-shift, closing, restroom, folding-area, and machine-check standards.
  • Improve wash-dry-fold intake, order accuracy, turnaround communication, and quality control.
  • Review KPIs weekly to spot patterns before they become customer or revenue issues.
  • Coach attendance, punctuality, accountability, task ownership, and customer service consistency.
60-Day Output: Documented standards + trained team rhythm + weekly KPI review.
Days 61–90

Grow + Prove

Use the new operating rhythm to grow service awareness, reduce leakage, and prove GM value.

  • Promote wash-dry-fold, pickup/delivery awareness, customer reviews, and repeat-customer loyalty.
  • Reduce repeat complaints, avoidable refunds, machine downtime, and supply waste.
  • Match staffing to traffic patterns so labor supports the busiest and most service-heavy periods.
  • Deliver a GM report with wins, risks, numbers, lessons learned, and next-quarter priorities.
90-Day Output: Growth report + next-quarter improvement plan.
Store Floor Standards Clean layout, clear walkways, machine readiness, and customer confidence.
Wash & Fold Team Flow Organized intake, folding, quality checks, and service consistency.
Customer Experience Friendly service, repeat visits, positive reviews, and trust.

Use daily walkthroughs, restroom checks, folding-area checks, and complaint tracking to raise standards.
